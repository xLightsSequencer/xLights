Use Effect Duration In Shaders

The Shader effect makes the length of the effect available to the shader code as one of its variables, alongside the other values it passes in.

Inside your shader you can read the effect duration and use it in your math, for example to scale animation speed or to drive an element that should complete exactly as the effect ends.

This gives shader authors a clean way to write effects that adapt to however long you stretch them on the grid, instead of hard-coding a fixed time and hoping it lines up.
