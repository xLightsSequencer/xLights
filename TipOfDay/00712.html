Shader Events On A Timing Track

Some shaders can react to a timing track, firing an event at each timing mark instead of running on a fixed cycle. The Lightning shader is a good example to study.

In the shader effect's settings you point it at a timing track, and the shader then triggers in time with those marks. With lightning, that means a strike lands on the beats you have laid down.

Tying a shader to your timing makes it feel deliberate and musical rather than random. It is a nice way to get dramatic, beat-driven moments out of the shader effects.
