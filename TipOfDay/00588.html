The Multiply Blending Mode

When you stack effects on layers, the blending mode decides how the upper layer combines with the one below. Multiply is one of those modes, and it multiplies the colors of the two layers together.

You choose it from the blending mode dropdown on an effect's layer. Because multiplying by a darker color darkens the result, it is great for using one layer as a mask or shadow over another, letting the lower layer show through only where the top layer is bright.

It is worth experimenting with on a two layer effect. Multiply can create rich, textured looks that you cannot get by simply laying one effect on top of another.
