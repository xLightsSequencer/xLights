Paste Puts Effects Back Where They Came From

When you copy a selection of effects and paste them, xLights now lines them up using their original times instead of bunching them all up at wherever your cursor happens to be.

This matters most when you copy a spread-out selection, say a few effects scattered across several seconds, then paste it onto another model or another part of the timeline. The gaps and overlaps between the effects are preserved, so the paste looks like the original selection rather than a squashed-together copy.

If you're moving a whole section of choreography between models, this means a lot less manual repositioning after the paste.
