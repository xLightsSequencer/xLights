Invert Channels In DMX Mapping

When mapping DMX channels you can invert a channel so its value runs in the opposite direction. A low value becomes high and a high value becomes low for that channel.

This is exactly what you need when a fixture's movement, dimming, or color wheel runs backward compared to how you sequenced it. Rather than re-sequencing, you invert the channel and the fixture responds the way you expect.

It is a quick fix for mismatched fixtures and saves you from reworking effects just because one device reads its DMX values in reverse.
