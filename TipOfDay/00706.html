Generic Serial Output

xLights includes a generic serial output type for driving controllers over a serial port that does not match one of the named protocols. You add it like any other controller in the setup tab and point it at the right serial port.

It lets you send raw channel data down a serial connection, which covers older or unusual hardware that still expects a simple stream of bytes.

If you have a controller that is not in the standard list but speaks a basic serial protocol, this is often the way to get it talking to xLights without special support being built in.
