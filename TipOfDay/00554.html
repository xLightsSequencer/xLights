Exclude Channels From Dimming And Gamma

An output processor can exclude specific channels from brightness dimming and gamma correction. You add it to your outputs so the chosen channels pass through untouched.

This is important when some of your channels are not driving lights at all, such as DMX channels that set a position, a speed, or a mode on a device. Applying a dimming curve or gamma to those would corrupt the value you actually want to send.

By excluding those channels, you can keep brightness and gamma adjustments on your pixels while leaving control channels exactly as your sequence intends.
