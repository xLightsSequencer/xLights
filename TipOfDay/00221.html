Create Submodels From States And Faces

You can turn the groups of nodes you defined in States and Faces into submodels without redrawing those selections by hand.

When editing a model that has States or Faces set up, look for the option to create submodels from them. Each state or face region becomes its own submodel that you can then sequence directly.

This saves a lot of tedious node picking and keeps everything consistent, since the submodels match the exact areas you already mapped out for your singing faces or state definitions.
