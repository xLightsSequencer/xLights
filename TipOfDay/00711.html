Different Coloured Eyes On Faces

When you define a node-range face, the eyes can be split into separate parts so each can be a different colour. You assign the eye node ranges in the face definition just like the mouth and outline.

This lets you do things like blue irises with white around them, or give a character coloured eyes that stand out from the rest of the face.

It adds a lot of character to singing faces and matrix portraits, since the eyes are often what people look at first and a splash of colour there really brings the face to life.
