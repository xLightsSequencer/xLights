Name Your Model And Submodel Layers

You can give display names to the individual layers of a model or submodel. Instead of a plain row number, each layer can carry a label that describes what it actually is.

On a complex prop like a servo-driven figure or a multi-channel DMX fixture, naming the layers makes the sequencer rows far easier to read, so you can tell at a glance which row controls which part.

It pays off most on models where the layers map to physical things like an arm, a head tilt, or a color wheel, where a meaningful name beats counting rows every time.
